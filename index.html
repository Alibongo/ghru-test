GHRU

A global network of partners building capacity for genomic surveillance of AMR.

Early warning of emergence and monitoring spread of AMR.

Open data for intervention and action.

Within the GHRU we are:

    Developing capacity for locally-led research projects in LMICs and sampling frameworks to address national public health and research needs.

  • Providing actionable data at national and international levels to identify high-risk lineages of bacterial pathogens and determinants of antibiotic resistance, and open-access tools to understand their distribution and spread.
  • Providing resourcing and training for local capacity and expertise to ensure ownership of outputs and utility for action.
  • Building a network of laboratories and experts across strategically important countries from which to expand to similar local contexts elsewhere

  1. Capacity Building

    Laboratory Infrastructure - We will enhance local capacity in national unit laboratories and equip each with capacity to undertake WGS of pathogens locally, and also regionally. Each national unit laboratory represents a network of AMR sentinel sites involved in surveillance within each country

    Training: In conjunction with WTAC we will deliver training courses and materials for utility at each national unit as well as international training.

    Data Infrastructure: Building expertise in data interpretation through easy-to-use tools will enable each location to further develop local research agendas and to feed data into global monitoring of particular pathogens between sites.

  2. National Context

    Data, made available for national utility through open data platforms (eg WGSA and Microreact) and the linking of relevant epidemiological and contextual data will enable further understanding to targetted infection control.

  3. Structured
    Pathogen Surveys

    Genomic surveillance of key pathogens will be undertaken to enable better understanding of the population and evolutionary biology of target organisms. Focus will be made on local priorities with respect to current AMR targets. Phenotypic surveillance combined with genomic surveillance will provide routes to intervention.

  4. Global Open Data

    Data will be made available for international utility through open data platforms (eg WGSA and Microreact) and public access (eg ENA). The linking of epidemiological and contextual data will enable the ongoing combination of data from all national units and sentinal sites to feed into a broader understanding of global patterns of AMR.
